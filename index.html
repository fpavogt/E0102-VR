Welcome!

Welcome to the website dedicated to the Virtual Reality (VR) application E0102-VR.
This application was developed between June and September 2018 at the Chilean headquarters of the European Southern Observatory (ESO) in Santiago. It was designed to explore the potential of VR for the visualization and scientific characterization of observational datasets. The specific scientific goal of E0102-VR is to help characterize the complex 3D structure of the oxygen-bright ejecta in the supernova remnant 1E 0102.2-7219, located in the Small Magellanic Cloud. These ejecta correspond to the outer-layers of a massive star that exploded as a core-collapse supernova ~2050 years ago. As a result, its outer-layers were expelled into space at a few 1000 km/s, giving rise to the complex filamentary structure we see today in the system. One key aspect of these ejecta is that they have been roughly moving ballistically (that is, with a constant speed) since the supernova explosion. Using this fact, we can easily convert the radial velocity of each ejecta knot into a physical depth. If you can measure the radial velocity of all the knots, you can then assemble a 3D map of the structure: that is, the real map of the star chunks as we see them today. Once you have this 3D map, you can then visualize it using the interactive plot below. Or use our E0102-VR application to fully immerse yourself in the system.
